What SignalForge Is

SignalForge is a distributed radio monitoring platform. A hub ingests and plays back calls. A CLI recorder uploads audio from your scanner, SDR, or repeater setup. A native mobile app (SignalForgeHub on iOS and Android) lets you monitor and push-to-talk from the field. Hubs can run online, fully off-grid, or federate with trusted peers through SignalHub.

Listen

Live monitor playback, embeddable public players, and background audio on mobile.

Contribute

Point the SignalForge CLI at a folder or stream and upload calls to any hub you trust.

Operate

Run your own Compose stack, own your PostgreSQL data, and stay off-grid when you need to.

Coordinate

Push-to-talk and dispatcher broadcast inside radio sets — permissions controlled per hub account.

Why We're Here

This started with a simple idea: Yukon deserved a public GMRS repeater — a reliable community radio hub that would keep neighborhoods connected when cell towers go dark and power grids fail. We put together the proposal, found the tower, reached out to the city.

The city didn't answer. The tower owner went quiet. The project stalled.

Getting ghosted didn't kill the idea. It sharpened it. If we couldn't own the infrastructure, we'd build something that didn't need it.

SignalForge Hub is what came next — a software-defined monitoring platform where community members contribute scanner, repeater, SDR, or streamed audio sources. No tower lease. No committee. Every person who runs a recorder expands the network.

The original goal was a repeater at every Yukon landmark — a cohesive signal network stitched across the city. The hardware approach hit walls. The software approach has no walls.

The city didn't give us the tower. So we built something that doesn't need one.

Who Builds It

SignalForge is built by projectseven co ltd in Yukon, Oklahoma. The platform is community infrastructure — operators run their own cells, contributors feed audio, and listeners tune in from the web or mobile app.

Want to contribute a scanner source, run a hub, or list your cell in the directory? Reach out — especially if you're operating a field cell and want federation or directory listing with Yukon.
